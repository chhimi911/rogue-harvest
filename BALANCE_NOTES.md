# Rogue Harvest Balance Notes

## Summary

The MVP is beatable and not obviously broken. A deterministic priority playthrough cleared the run with 44/72 HP, which suggests the first build may lean forgiving when the player uses poison, vulnerable, and efficient attacks well.

## Fight Feel

- Mold Mite works as a first enemy and gives the player time to learn basic attacks/block.
- Rot Grub introduces poison pressure without being overwhelming.
- Wilted Vinecrawler reinforces vulnerable/block patterns.
- Baron Botrytis has boss-scale HP and stronger intent values, but is still beatable with a straightforward strategy.

## Card Notes

- Poison is valuable because Rot Kiss and Night Bloom create delayed damage and trigger Berry Burst.
- Dodge is strong and clear mechanically, especially with zero-cost Compost Feint and Ghostberry Gambit.
- Block matters, but Bramble Cloak plus Dodge can sometimes make early damage feel very manageable.
- Attack rewards are understandable; rare cards feel useful without immediately ending the run.
- Juiced Up is likely one of the best rewards because it costs 0, gains energy, and draws.

## Relic Notes

- Golden Trowel is simple and immediately useful.
- Moonwater Flask helps recovery and contributed to the successful playthrough.
- Relic rewards are deterministic, which is good for testing but less exciting for replayability.

## Recommended Tuning Experiments

- Track average HP after each node across 5-10 manual runs before changing numbers.
- If the boss feels too easy for real players, raise boss HP modestly or make Blight Decree appear earlier.
- If new players lose too often, leave numbers alone and first improve status explanations.
